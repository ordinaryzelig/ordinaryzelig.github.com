Why testing matters

There is no such thing as bug-free code. If you were an online bank, how confident are you that every transaction is working like it is designed to? Can you afford to have a bug? With automated tests, you have code that verifies your application is working as expected. Most good programmers nowadays are thoroughly testing their code. It helps with development, and it helps when things go wrong, like things eventually do. Good test coverage can help fix bugs when they pop up. Tests will also help you make sure that fixing a bug here won't cause another bug way over there. If you don't have tests, your bugs will be much more difficult to squash, costing you more time and more money.

Does my rate sound too expensive?

While you can find developers around the world that will work for less, it is important to consider quality and what it is worth to you. If you go the cheap route, you could get cheap code that might seem to work but is a constant headache. Your development velocity could feel like swimming through thick sludge. If it's bad enough, you might even need to start over. And what happens when things go wrong? Do your cheap devs have tests to verify their code? "Cheap and fast" could take several times longer, costing several times more, not to mention the headaches. So don't just compare rates by their numbers. What is it worth to you to do it the right way?
